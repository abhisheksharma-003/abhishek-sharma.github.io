Vinton Cerf

One of the "fathers of the Internet"

Here's a time line of Vint Cerf's life:

  • 1943 - Born in New Haven, Connecticut.
  • 1965 - Received a B.S. in Mathematics from Stanford University.
  • 1972 - Earned his Ph.D. in computer science from UCLA.
  • 1973 - Co-designed the TCP/IP protocol with Robert Kahn, which became the foundational communication protocol of the Internet.
  • 1997 - Received the National Medal of Technology from President Bill Clinton.
  • 2004 - Awarded the Turing Award, the "Nobel Prize of Computing."
  • 2005 - Joined Google as Vice President and Chief Internet Evangelist.

"The internet is a reflection of our society and that mirror is going to be reflecting what we see. If we do not like what we see in that mirror the problem is not to fix the mirror, we have to fix society."

-- Vinton Cerf
